## What I understood
Vector search works by converting text into embeddings (numerical vectors) and comparing their meaning instead of exact words.

Instead of matching keywords, it measures how close two vectors are in space. This allows it to find related content even if the wording is different.

When using cosine distance:

- A score close to 0.0 means the vectors are very similar
- A score closer to 1.0 means they are more different

Also, ordering by ASC returns the most relevant results first when using distance.
